## Process

- **Commits:** 20.02, 21.02, 27.02, 28.02; 01.03, 02.03, 03.03, 04.03.
- **Approximate time** on those days: on average about 5 hours, some days up to 8 hours, some less – with breaks. Hard to give exact figures. Alongside other projects, I invested a lot of time in this one.

**Learning:** I should track my time better so it’s clear how much went into each feature. It would also make sense to **commit more often**, rather than batching changes into large chunks.

---

## Hardest parts

1. **Gmail sending with booking** – sending confirmation emails (SMTP, Gmail app password). I used AI help.
2. **Page design** – UI/UX and visual design. I used AI help.
3. **Placing tables on the floor plan** – making tables active, resizing, rotation and positioning on the coordinate system. **Done by me**, with theoretical input from someone who had done something similar in their thesis.

---

## What could have been done better

- **Tests:** I could have written code and tests in parallel. This remains an important area to improve.
- **Bugs and errors:** there may be issues in the code. Time constraints mean not everything may be 100% precise.
- **Commits:** more frequent, smaller commits would have made history easier to follow and rollbacks simpler.

---

## What could be added

- **Booking cancellation:** Customers could cancel bookings easily.
- **Payment system:** Option to pay for a booking.
- **Localization:** Multi-language support.
- **Statistics:** Some form of statistics display.
- **QR code scanning:** Functionality to scan QR codes.
- There is a lot of room for further development.

## Architecture and my style

- It’s not mandatory to structure code in such detail (separate DTOs, requests, responses, layers), but **it matches my style and skills**. It demonstrates knowledge and keeps the code organized and maintainable.
- I have strong domain modelling and planning skills.
- I committed from different branches.

## Summary

I am very satisfied with the work and know there is still a lot to learn. At the same time I feel I made significant progress independently with this project. It’s another solid project in my portfolio, and I’m very grateful and happy for the opportunity.
